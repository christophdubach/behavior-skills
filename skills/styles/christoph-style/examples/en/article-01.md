# Interview for IAAP EU

## Why did you become certified?
The rapid pace of technological advancements and evolving design trends can easily push accessibility considerations aside. And there’s this persistent myth that design, usability, and accessibility are at odds with one another, and that ensuring accessibility leads to slower and more costly development. For a long time, it wasn’t easy to get people to open their ears to this topic, neither from the client side nor internally.
With the introduction of the European Accessibility Act, the discussion around accessibility has changed significantly. Today everyone wants to talk about accessibility and is actively seeking expert advice. I was looking for a way to make my knowledge and experience more visible and to be able to share it effectively.
Given Unic’s international client base, having a globally recognised certification like CPACC is a huge advantage. It provides credibility and builds trust with our clients - a key factor in applying the Shift Left approach: integrating accessibility from the start and ensuring it’s a fundamental part of the development process.

## What has the certification meant to your career/to you personally?
For my career, the CPACC certification has certainly opened new doors. It’s a clear confirmation of my expertise in this important field. Within Unic, it has strengthened my position as a go-to resource for accessibility, allowing me to support projects across various phases. It will also play an increasingly vital role in engaging with potential clients, especially those operating internationally. These clients need confidence in our understanding of global accessibility standards.
For me personally, the certification process was incredibly enriching. My previous experience was primarily based on knowledge of technical requirements, particularly WCAG. The in-depth preparation naturally led me to delve deeper into the relevant social and legal contexts that shape inclusive design. This made me a more effective professional and increased my awareness and empathy for the diverse ways people interact with technology.

## What would you say professional certification means for society/our community at large?
The certification establishes a recognised standard of competency and makes accessibility in digital product design and development a practical experience, rather than just a theoretical ideal. As technology increasingly shapes our lives, accessibility is crucial for everyone, regardless of ability.
I believe that certification creates a common ground and enables collaboration and knowledge sharing within our community. It also helps elevate the entire field and strengthens our collective voice in advocating for digital inclusion.

## Any additional thoughts on IAAP membership?
It offers a valuable connection to a global community of professionals with diverse perspectives and experiences. As someone actively involved in open source projects and the organisation of events, I know the importance of collaboration and knowledge sharing. I am currently preparing for the WAS certification and I am confident that the resources and networking opportunities will be invaluable.
